# Chapter Overview

**Chapter Objective:** By the end of this chapter, learners will be able to describe a typical day from morning to night, using reflexive verbs and adverbs of frequency.

**Communicative Goals:**
- Describe a morning routine.
- Use reflexive verbs correctly in the present tense.
- Describe a work or school day.
- Describe an evening and bedtime routine.
- Express how often something happens.
- Describe someone else's daily routine.

**CEFR Can-Do Statements:**
- I can describe my morning routine.
- I can use reflexive verbs to talk about daily actions.
- I can describe my work or school day.
- I can describe my evening and bedtime routine.
- I can say how often I do something using adverbs of frequency.
- I can describe someone else's typical day.

**Grammar Objectives:**
- **Reflexive verbs in the present tense**, built progressively across the chapter (je me / tu te → il/elle se → nous nous / vous vous → ils/elles se), using **se réveiller** ("to wake up") as the anchor verb. This chapter also formally teaches the **full present-tense conjugation pattern of regular *-er* verbs**, resolving several verbs previously taught only as chunks (*parler*, *aimer*, *adorer*, *détester*, *porter* — see the Grammar Ledger).
- **Adverbs of frequency** (*toujours, souvent, parfois, rarement, jamais*) and their placement in a sentence.

**Vocabulary Domains:** Daily activities and routines, time-of-day expressions (relative, not clock-based — exact time-telling is reserved for Chapter 7 per the Skeleton).

**Cultural Notes:** Meal times in France tend to be more fixed and structured than in some other cultures — lunch, in particular, is often a genuine, unhurried break rather than eaten at one's desk. This will be explored further in Chapter 5 (Food & Dining); this chapter simply notes the daily rhythm it creates.

**Prerequisites:** Chapters 1–3. This chapter assumes full command of *être*, *avoir*, adjective agreement (gender, number, placement), the complete possessive adjective system, and the *tu/vous* distinction. None of this is re-taught.

**Learning Outcomes:** A learner completing this chapter can narrate a full day, start to finish, for themselves or someone else, using correctly conjugated reflexive verbs and expressing how often they do things.

**Estimated Completion Time:** 3–3.5 hours across all eight lessons.

# LESSON 1

# Lesson Information
**Lesson Number:** 1
**Lesson Title:** Morning Routine
**Anchor Skill:** Reading (R)

**Grammar Focus:** Reflexive verbs — introduction: the reflexive pronoun concept, and *je me réveille / tu te réveilles*.

**Estimated Time:** 25–30 minutes

---

# Warm-Up
Think about the first three things you do every morning, in order. In French, many everyday actions like these are expressed with a special type of verb — one where the action "reflects back" onto the person doing it. Today you'll meet this pattern for the first time.

---

# Lesson Explanation
A **reflexive verb** describes an action the subject does to themselves. In French, this is shown with a small reflexive pronoun placed just before the verb: **je me réveille** ("I wake myself up," naturally translated as "I wake up"), **tu te réveilles** ("you wake up").

The reflexive pronoun changes with the subject (*me* for *je*, *te* for *tu*), while the verb ending itself follows the regular pattern of an *-er* verb (more on this full pattern in Lesson 2). For now, focus on the two forms you need: **je me** and **tu te**.

**Se lever** ("to get up") and **se laver** ("to wash oneself") follow the identical pattern: **je me lève**, **tu te lèves**; **je me lave**, **tu te laves**. **S'habiller** ("to get dressed") begins with a vowel sound, so *me/te* contract: **je m'habille**, **tu t'habilles**.

**D'abord** ("first," in a sequence) helps order a routine: *D'abord, je me réveille.*

---

# Vocabulary

| French | English | Pronunciation Guidance | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| se réveiller | to wake up | suh ray-vay-YAY | Je me réveille à sept heures. |
| se lever | to get up | suh luh-VAY | Tu te lèves tôt ? |
| se laver | to wash (oneself) | suh lah-VAY | Je me lave le matin. |
| s'habiller | to get dressed | sah-bee-YAY | Tu t'habilles vite ! |
| le matin | the morning | luh mah-TAN | Le matin, je me réveille tôt. |
| tôt | early | TOH | Je me lève tôt. |
| d'abord | first (in sequence) | dah-BOR | D'abord, je me réveille. |

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson introduces the reflexive verb structure using only the *je* and *tu* forms, keeping the full paradigm build gradual, per FrancPrep's standard grammar sequencing.

**Formation (so far):**
| Pronoun | Reflexive Pronoun | Se réveiller |
|---|---|---|
| je | me (m' before a vowel sound) | je me réveille |
| tu | te (t' before a vowel sound) | tu te réveilles |

**Usage:** The reflexive pronoun always sits directly before the conjugated verb, never separated from it.

**Examples:**
1. Je me réveille tôt le matin.
2. Tu te lèves à quelle heure ?
3. D'abord, je me lave, puis je m'habille.

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *Je réveille* (missing the reflexive pronoun) → ✅ *Je me réveille.*
- ❌ *Je me habille* → ✅ *Je m'habille* (elision before a vowel sound).

**Mini Drills:**
1. Complete: __________ (je, se réveiller) à six heures.
2. Ask a friend when they get up: __________ te lèves-tu ?

# LESSON 2

# Lesson Information
**Lesson Number:** 2
**Lesson Title:** Reflexive Verbs in Action
**Anchor Skill:** Writing (W)

**Grammar Focus:** Reflexive verbs — extension: *il/elle se*; formal introduction of the complete regular *-er* verb ending pattern (-e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent).

**Estimated Time:** 25–30 minutes

---

# Warm-Up
You've used *je parle*, *j'aime*, and *je porte* since earlier chapters without seeing the full pattern behind them. Today, that pattern is finally made explicit — and it's the same pattern behind every reflexive verb you're learning in this chapter.

---

# Lesson Explanation
Regular *-er* verbs (the largest group of French verbs, including *parler*, *aimer*, *adorer*, *détester*, *porter* — all introduced earlier as chunks — and reflexive verbs like *se réveiller*) share one consistent present-tense ending pattern. Take off the *-er* from the infinitive, and add:

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: parler |
|---|---|---|
| je | -e | je parle |
| tu | -es | tu parles |
| il/elle | -e | il/elle parle |
| nous | -ons | nous parlons |
| vous | -ez | vous parlez |
| ils/elles | -ent | ils/elles parlent |

This means every *-er* verb you've already used as a chunk (*je parle*, *j'aime*, *j'adore*, *je déteste*, *je porte*) now makes full sense as part of one predictable system — you can now confidently produce *tu parles*, *il aime*, *nous portons*, and so on.

For reflexive verbs, the same ending pattern applies to the verb itself — only the reflexive pronoun in front changes: **il se réveille**, **elle se réveille** (note: *il/elle* use *se*, not *me/te*).

---

# Vocabulary

| French | English | Pronunciation Guidance | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| se brosser les dents | to brush one's teeth | suh broh-say lay DAHN | Il se brosse les dents le matin. |
| se coiffer | to do one's hair | suh kwah-FAY | Elle se coiffe avant l'école. |
| ensuite | then / next | ahn-SWEET | Je me lave, ensuite je m'habille. |
| puis | then (similar to ensuite) | PWEE | Puis, il part au travail. |
| rapidement | quickly | rah-peed-MAHN | Il s'habille rapidement. |

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson names the full *-er* verb ending system explicitly and extends reflexive verbs to *il/elle*.

**Formation — Reflexive (so far):**
| Pronoun | Reflexive Pronoun | Se réveiller |
|---|---|---|
| je | me | je me réveille |
| tu | te | tu te réveilles |
| il / elle | se | il/elle se réveille |

**Formation — Full regular -er pattern (now explicit):**
Stem (infinitive minus *-er*) + *-e / -es / -e / -ons / -ez / -ent*.

**Usage:** This pattern now retroactively explains every *-er* verb chunk used since Chapter 2 — *parler*, *aimer*, *adorer*, *détester*, *porter* — all fully regular.

**Examples:**
1. Léo se réveille tard ; il se brosse les dents rapidement.
2. Nous parlons français. (recall from Ch2 — now fully explained)
3. Awa aime le café ; elle en boit chaque matin. *(boit previewed as receptive vocabulary only — irregular verb, not yet taught)*

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *Il se réveilles* → ✅ *Il se réveille* (the *il/elle* ending is *-e*, not *-es* — don't confuse it with *tu*.)
- ❌ *Elle me coiffe* → ✅ *Elle se coiffe* (the reflexive pronoun must match the subject, not stay fixed as *me*.)

**Mini Drills:**
1. Complete: Camille __________ (se brosser les dents) après le petit-déjeuner.
2. Complete using the pattern: Il __________ (parler) très bien français.

# Lesson Explanation
This lesson extends reflexive verbs to **nous nous** and **vous vous**: **nous nous réveillons**, **vous vous réveillez**. Notice the reflexive pronoun appears twice in a row for *nous* and *vous* — this looks unusual at first but is entirely regular.

**Une pause** ("a break"), **le bureau** ("the office"), and **l'après-midi** ("the afternoon") round out the vocabulary needed to describe a workday.

---

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson extends the reflexive paradigm to the plural/formal persons.

**Formation:**
| Pronoun | Reflexive Pronoun | Se réveiller |
|---|---|---|
| nous | nous | nous nous réveillons |
| vous | vous | vous vous réveillez |

**Usage:** Even though *nous* and *vous* each appear twice, this is entirely regular — the first is the subject pronoun, the second is the reflexive pronoun, and neither is dropped.

**Examples:**
1. Nous nous réveillons tôt le lundi.
2. Vous vous levez à quelle heure, Madame Lambert ?
3. Avant le travail, nous nous préparons rapidement.

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *Nous réveillons* (dropping the reflexive pronoun) → ✅ *Nous nous réveillons.*
- ❌ *Vous vous levez* mispronounced by merging the two "vous" sounds → keep both syllables clearly distinct in speech.

**Mini Drills:**
1. Complete: __________ __________ (nous, se lever) à sept heures.
2. Complete formally: __________ __________ (vous, se réveiller) tôt, Monsieur Roy ?

# Lesson Explanation
**Ils/elles se** completes the reflexive paradigm: **ils/elles se couchent** ("they go to bed"). The reflexive pronoun *se* is the same one already used for *il/elle*, so this final step is a small one.

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson completes the reflexive verb present-tense paradigm.

**Formation — Complete Paradigm (Se réveiller / Se coucher):**
| Pronoun | Se réveiller | Se coucher |
|---|---|---|
| je | je me réveille | je me couche |
| tu | tu te réveilles | tu te couches |
| il / elle | il/elle se réveille | il/elle se couche |
| nous | nous nous réveillons | nous nous couchons |
| vous | vous vous réveillez | vous vous couchez |
| ils / elles | ils/elles se réveillent | ils/elles se couchent |

**Usage:** *Ils/elles se* uses the *-ent* ending, consistent with the regular *-er* pattern taught in Lesson 2.

**Examples:**
1. Léo et Nora se couchent tard le vendredi.
2. Camille et Awa se détendent après le travail.
3. Ils se douchent avant de se coucher.

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *Ils se couche* → ✅ *Ils se couchent* (don't forget the *-ent* ending.)
- ❌ Confusing *se coucher* (to go to bed) with *dormir* (to sleep, a different, not-yet-taught verb) — *se coucher* describes the action of getting into bed, not the state of being asleep.

**Mini Drills:**

# Lesson Information

**Anchor Skill:** Reading (R)
**Lesson Objectives:** Express how often something happens using adverbs of frequency.
**Grammar Focus:** Adverbs of frequency and their placement in a sentence.
**Vocabulary Focus:** toujours, souvent, parfois, rarement, ne...jamais, chaque jour
**Estimated Time:** 25 minutes

---

# Warm-Up
You can now describe an entire day — but how often do you actually follow that routine? Every day? Only sometimes? This lesson gives you the tools to say so.

---

# Lesson Explanation
Adverbs of frequency describe how often something happens, ranked from most to least frequent: **toujours** (always) > **souvent** (often) > **parfois** (sometimes) > **rarement** (rarely) > **jamais** (never, used with *ne*, following the same negation pattern from Chapter 3).

**Placement:** these adverbs are placed directly **after** the conjugated verb, not before it as in English: **Je me lève toujours tôt** ("I always get up early" — literally "I get up always early").

**Chaque jour** ("every day") behaves differently — like most time expressions, it typically goes at the beginning or end of the sentence, not immediately after the verb: *Chaque jour, je me réveille à sept heures.*

---

# Vocabulary

| French | English | Pronunciation Guidance | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| toujours | always | too-ZHOOR | Je me lève toujours tôt. |
| souvent | often | soo-VAHN | Il se douche souvent le soir. |
| parfois | sometimes | par-FWAH | Parfois, je me couche tard. |
| rarement | rarely | rar-MAHN | Elle se lève rarement après huit heures. |
| ne...jamais | never | nuh...zhah-MEH | Je ne me lève jamais après neuf heures. |
| chaque jour | every day | shahk ZHOOR | Chaque jour, je me réveille tôt. |

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson introduces adverbs of frequency and their fixed placement rule.

**Formation — Placement:**
- ✅ Subject + verb + **frequency adverb**: *Je me réveille toujours tôt.*
- ❌ *Toujours je me réveille tôt.* (incorrect placement, even though it would be natural in English: "I always wake up early")

**Formation — Negative frequency (jamais):**
*ne* + verb + *jamais* (same structure as *ne...pas* from Chapter 3): *Je ne me couche jamais avant vingt-deux heures.*

**Usage:** *Chaque jour* is more flexible in placement (beginning or end of sentence) than the other frequency words in this lesson, which sit fixed after the verb.

**Examples:**
1. Nous nous levons souvent tôt le week-end.
2. Monsieur Roy ne se couche jamais après minuit.
3. Chaque jour, Awa se réveille à six heures et demie.

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *Toujours je me lève tôt.* → ✅ *Je me lève toujours tôt.*
- ❌ *Je jamais me couche tard.* → ✅ *Je ne me couche jamais tard.*

**Mini Drills:**

2. Complete: Elle __________ __________ tard. (never goes to bed)
