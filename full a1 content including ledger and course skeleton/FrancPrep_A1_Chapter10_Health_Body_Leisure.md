---

# Lesson Explanation
**J'ai mal à** + body part means "I have a pain in..." or, more naturally, "my [body part] hurts": **j'ai mal à la tête** ("I have a headache"), **j'ai mal au dos** ("my back hurts" — note *à* + *le* contracts to *au*, the same pattern from Chapter 8's prepositions).

**La fièvre** (fever), **la toux** (a cough), **être malade** (to be sick), and **se sentir bien/mal** (to feel well/unwell, a reflexive verb recycled from Chapter 4's pattern) complete this lesson's vocabulary.

---

# Vocabulary

| French | English | Pronunciation Guidance | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| j'ai mal à | I have a pain in / my ... hurts | zhay mal ah | J'ai mal à la tête. |
| la fièvre | fever | lah FYEHVR | J'ai de la fièvre. |
| la toux | a cough | lah TOO | Il a une toux depuis hier. |
| être malade | to be sick | ehtr mah-LAD | Je suis malade aujourd'hui. |
| se sentir bien/mal | to feel well/unwell | suh sahn-TEER byan/mal | Je ne me sens pas bien. |

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson teaches *avoir mal à*, with attention to its contraction with *le*.

**Formation:**
| à + article | Becomes | Example |
|---|---|---|
| à + le | au | j'ai mal au dos |
| à + la | à la (no change) | j'ai mal à la tête |
| à + l' | à l' (elision) | j'ai mal à l'estomac |
| à + les | aux | j'ai mal aux jambes |

**Usage:** This is the same contraction pattern seen with *de* (Chapter 8) — *à* also contracts with *le* and *les*, but not *la*.

**Examples:**
1. J'ai mal à la gorge et je tousse.
2. Il a mal au dos depuis le sport d'hier.
3. Elle a de la fièvre et ne se sent pas bien.

**Common Mistakes:**
- ❌ *J'ai mal à le dos* → ✅ *J'ai mal au dos* (contraction is required).
- ❌ *Je suis malade au dos* (mixing structures) → ✅ Keep *être malade* (general) separate from *avoir mal à* (specific body part).

**Mini Drills:**
1. Complete: J'ai mal __________ jambes. (à + les)
2. Complete: Il a mal __________ tête. (à + la)

# Lesson Information
**Lesson Number:** 5
**Lesson Title:** Faire vs Jouer
**Anchor Skill:** Reading (R)
**Lesson Objectives:** Correctly distinguish *faire* and *jouer* for different activities.
**Grammar Focus:** *Faire* vs. *jouer* — the genuine rule governing which verb pairs with which activity.
**Vocabulary Focus:** *(grammar-focused lesson; recycles Lesson 4's sports/hobbies vocabulary)*
**Estimated Time:** 25–30 minutes

---

# Warm-Up
You now have the vocabulary. Today you'll learn the real rule that decides whether an activity uses *faire* or *jouer* — a genuine, important distinction that trips up many learners.

---

# Lesson Explanation
French uses **faire de** (+ *le/la/l'/les*, contracted to *du/de la/de l'/des*) for most sports and physical activities without a ball or opposing team: **faire du vélo**, **faire de la natation**, **faire de la danse**.

French uses **jouer à** (+ contracted article: *au/à la/à l'/aux*) specifically for **games with a ball or opponent**, especially sports played against someone: **jouer au foot**, **jouer au tennis**.

**The rule of thumb:** if it's a game with a ball, opponent, or clear rules of play (foot, tennis, cartes/cards), use *jouer à*. If it's a physical activity or discipline without that structure (cycling, swimming, painting, dance), use *faire de*.

---

# Vocabulary

*(This lesson recycles Lesson 4's vocabulary; no new standalone items beyond the grammar structure itself.)*

---

# Grammar

**Explanation:** This lesson teaches the *faire/jouer* distinction directly.

**Formation:**
| Structure | Contraction Pattern | Used For |
|---|---|---|
| faire de + article | du/de la/de l'/des | Physical activities without ball/opponent (vélo, natation, danse, peinture) |
| jouer à + article | au/à la/à l'/aux | Games with ball/opponent (foot, tennis, cartes) |

**Usage:** This mirrors the *à*-contraction pattern from Lesson 2 (*au, à la, à l', aux*) and the *de*-contraction pattern from Chapter 8 (*du, de la, de l', des*) — both patterns you already know, now applied to a new distinction.

**Examples:**
1. Léo fait du vélo le week-end.
2. Monsieur Roy joue au tennis chaque samedi.
3. Awa fait de la natation, mais elle ne joue pas au foot.

**Common Mistakes:**
