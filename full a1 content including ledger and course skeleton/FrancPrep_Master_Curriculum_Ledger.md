## PREAMBLE & STANDING PRODUCTION RULES

Three chapters into production, several implicit practices have proven themselves and are now codified as permanent, binding rules for all future chapters — not stylistic defaults, but requirements to be checked against this ledger every time.

**Rule 1 — Progressive Verb Paradigm Construction.** When a chapter's grammar focus is a verb, its full present-tense paradigm is never taught in one lesson. It is built progressively, one or two persons per lesson, in this fixed order unless a specific communicative need requires otherwise: *je/tu → il/elle → nous/vous → ils/elles*. The lesson that completes the paradigm is logged as that verb's **Mastery Point** in the Grammar Ledger.

**Rule 2 — One New Grammar Concept Per Lesson, Maximum.** Every lesson introduces at most one new grammar point. Prior grammar is recycled freely and without re-explanation.

**Rule 3 — No Silent Vocabulary Duplication.** Before drafting any lesson, its planned vocabulary is checked against the Vocabulary Ledger. A word already taught as *active* is never reintroduced as new; it may be reused freely in dialogues and readings.

**Rule 4 — No Silent Grammar Scope Creep.** A chapter's grammar is confined to what the Curriculum Skeleton assigns it. If a lesson's communicative task genuinely requires a structure outside that scope (e.g., basic negation needed to teach "dislikes"), it is introduced only as a minimal, functional pattern, explicitly flagged in the lesson text as a limited exception, and logged in the Grammar Exceptions Ledger with a note on when it will be formally, fully taught.

**Rule 5 — Chunk-Before-Conjugation.** A verb may appear as a fixed lexical chunk (e.g., *je parle*, *j'aime*) before its formal conjugation lesson, provided it is tagged "chunk / receptive" in the Grammar Ledger until the chapter that formally teaches its conjugation arrives.

**Rule 6 — Recurring Character Cast (in effect from Chapter 4 onward).** All dialogues use the fixed recurring cast defined in the Character Ledger below, per Editorial Style Guide §6. See the **Character Ledger Audit Note** for how this applies retroactively to Chapters 1–3.

**Rule 7 — English Translation Support (A1–A2 only).** Every French Reading passage, Listening transcript, and Lesson 7 Integrated dialogue at **A1 and A2** includes a clearly labeled English Translation block immediately beneath the French text, formatted consistently so the platform layer can wrap it in a collapsible/toggle UI component without requiring content changes. **From B1 onward, no translations are provided** — comprehension is built entirely through context, glossed vocabulary, and comprehension questions, consistent with CEFR's expectation of growing learner independence at the "threshold" level. **Status: fully applied.** Chapters 1–3 were retrofitted after the rule was established (all Reading/Listening/Integrated passages and DELF assessment passages now carry translation blocks, matching Chapter 4's original treatment exactly). All four existing A1 chapters are now uniform under this rule; no backlog remains.

**Rule 8 — Mandatory Cross-Check Before and After Every Chapter.** Before drafting any new chapter, this Ledger is consulted in full: Vocabulary (no duplication), Grammar (correct scope + progressive sequencing per Rules 1–2), Communicative Functions (no redundant can-do introduction), Dialogue/Scenario balance, Character Ledger (recurring cast usage per Rule 6), Grammar Exceptions, and Cultural Notes. After the chapter is drafted, all seven ledgers are updated with that chapter's new entries, and a short audit note is added confirming the chapter was checked against the Constitution, Editorial Style Guide, and Master Template — not just this Ledger. A chapter is not considered complete until this update step is done, matching the same discipline as the Editorial Style Guide's own Quality Control checklist.
